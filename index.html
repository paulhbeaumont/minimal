Working papers

1. Building a Customer Base under Liquidity Constraints (pdf),
with Clémence Lenoir (CREST).
In the presence of liquidity constraints, firms face a trade-off between financing the working capital needs generated by existing customers and investing in the expansion of the customer base. We test for this idea and its implications by relying on the combination of a quasi-natural experiment and unique customer-level data sets. Our results strongly support the view that firms subject to long payment periods under-invest in the formation of a customer base.
Last presented at: GEP/CEPR Conference (2018), BFRF (2018), AFSE (2018), RIEF (2018), BYEC (2018), EEA-ESEM (2018*), EFA (2018*), SETC (2018*)
Press coverage: VoxFi

2. Build or Buy? Human Capital and Corporate Diversification (pdf),
with Camille Hébert (Paris Dauphine/Tilburg) and Victor Lyonnet (OSU).
Why do some firms enter a new market by acquiring an existing company operating in the market (external entry), while more than 90% of firms do so by building on their existing resources (internal entry)? Using a novel dataset merging French employer payrolls and information on M&A deals, we show that human capital acquisition is a key motive behind the decision to grow externally.
Last presented at: ECGC (2018), Mitsui Finance Symposium (2018), NFA (2018*), University of Texas Forum on Corporate Finance (2018*).
Best Ph.D. Paper at 2018 ECGC Workshop on Governance and Control
